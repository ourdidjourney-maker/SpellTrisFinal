How to play

SPELLTRIS is Tetris with words. Letters rain down — stack them so a run spells a real word and it clears like a line. Survive, spell, and chain for big scores.

The goal

Steer each falling letter and drop it where you want. Any run of letters that spells a dictionary word — reading left→right, right→left, top→bottom, or bottom→top — clears instantly. Let a column pile up to the ceiling and the run is over.

C-A-T sits in a row → it clears

Difficulty

Pick a preset on the start screen — it bundles several settings at once:

  • Easy — clears on 2-letter words, an even letter bag, a slow fall, the Tutor on, and a strong letter helper that steers the drops toward letters that actually complete words on your board, so there's always something to clear.
  • Medium — 3-letter minimum, the weighted (realistic) bag, normal speed, occasional letter help.
  • Hard — 4-letter minimum, weighted bag, fast, the next-piece preview hidden, and no letter help at all — pure luck of the draw.
  • Custom — set Board width, Tile size, Fall speed, Shortest word, and the letter pool yourself. Touching speed or shortest-word flips you to Custom.

Anti-Gravity mode

Pick Anti-Gravity as the Game mode on the start screen for a completely different feel. There's no gravity — each letter is fired in from an edge emitter and bounces around the board. Tap any + along the top, bottom, left, or right to choose which edge cell the next brick shoots in from. Steer the drifting brick with the ◀▶▲▼ thrusters (or the on-screen pad), then press Space or Shift to place it — it snaps to the nearest open cell and locks there, floating. Spell words across, down, or backwards exactly like normal and they clear, opening space back up.

Win: clear the entire board. Lose: the board floods — fills up with no open cell left for the next letter. So place only letters you can use: every brick is permanent until it's part of a word. The Tutor is especially handy here, since you can drop a letter anywhere it highlights.

Tucking under bricks

Press left/right toward a gap beneath an overhang and the piece slides over and drops into the pocket in one move — you don't have to fall past the brick first.

Press ◀ → the D tucks under the L

Delete a falling brick

Stuck with a letter you can't use? Press ◀ and ▶ together to vaporize the falling brick. It costs half your current score (50 pts → spend 25), and you need at least 2 points to do it. When you can afford it, the falling brick slowly pulses bright blue — toggle that glow off with the Delete FX button if you'd rather not see it.

Chains & scoring

Clearing a word drops the stack above it — if that spells another word, it clears too, and the combo multiplier climbs (×1.5 per step). Longer words and higher levels score far more. Every 6 words you level up and the fall speeds up.

Design targets — Main game only

Hit the Design button to open the Design Studio, paint a shape onto a copy of the board, and press Set target. Its red outline appears on your board. Now spell words through those cells — every cleared cell inside the shape fills with green lines (bricks sit right on top of them). Complete the whole shape and a dialog rewards you: clear the board or delete blocks. The Overlay button cycles the guide: full → outline only → off.

Red = your target outline · green = cells already solved · a real brick sits in the shape

When a word clears

Spell a word and those bricks don't just vanish — the cleared cells flash white-hot and cool like stamped metal, glowing amber → orange → deep red before fading into the dark, with wispy smoke curling up off them. The bricks themselves detach and tumble off the board, still glowing molten and cooling as they spin and fall. Then the stack above collapses into the gap. Pure celebration; it has no effect on scoring. Toggle the Smoke off if you like (the cooling glow stays either way), and if your device prefers reduced motion the bricks simply clear without the tumble.

Backwards-word bonus

Spell a word that's only valid read backwards — the tiles read RAF but clear as FAR — and you trigger a bonus. Play pauses, an amber banner appears, and you get to tap and delete a handful of bricks for free (as many as the word is long) with the same molten clear effect — a great way to dig yourself out of a jam. Words that are valid both directions (like TAR / RAT) don't count; it has to be a genuine backwards-only find. Turn the whole mechanic on or off with the Bonus button.

Auto-lock timer

Want more pressure? Flip the Timer button to give every falling brick a countdown — a small ⏱ clock shows above the board, and when it hits zero the brick locks itself in place wherever it is. Cycle Off → 30s → 15s → 8s. It works in both Main mode and Anti-Gravity, so you can turn a relaxed game into a race.

The "+" drop bar

The little + marks above the board pick which column new pieces fall from — click one to aim in real time. They rescale for any board width.

Cascade (auto-sweep drops)

Click Cascade to cycle through drop patterns where the spawn column marches across the board on its own — → left-to-right, ← right-to-left, or ⇄ bouncing back and forth like Knight Rider / falling rain. Each new brick spawns one column over from the last. Cascade overrides the manual + bar and Random while it's on.

The buttons

The control bar sits in two rows: game functions on top, and housekeeping (view, menu, help) below. A few are icons now — ☰ Menu, ⏸ Pause, ▶AI AI Play, and ▮▮▮ Hide UI.

  • ☰ Menu — return to the start screen to change mode, difficulty, or board settings and start a fresh run.
  • ⏸ Pause — pause / resume (also Esc).
  • ▮▮▮ Hide UI — collapse the whole control bar down to just this button; tap again to bring everything back.
  • ⇅ Button size — cycle the control-bar buttons through Small / Medium / Large for your screen.
  • Tutor — highlights every spot your current letter could complete a word. A coach, not autoplay.
  • Next — show or hide the upcoming letter for an extra challenge.
  • Bag — Even gives every letter equal odds; Weighted uses real letter frequencies (harder, more vowels/commons).
  • Random — pieces drop from a random column each time; the + bar turns red and locks.
  • Shotgun — blasts random locked bricks across the board with a screen shake. Instant chaos.
  • Cascade — cycles the auto-sweep drop patterns (→, ←, ⇄) so pieces march across the board on their own. See "Cascade" above.
  • Tic Tac — pieces become two connected bricks (side-by-side or stacked). Rotate with Enter or ⟳.
  • Timer — give each brick a countdown that auto-locks it (Off / 30s / 15s / 8s). See "Auto-lock timer" above.
  • Delete FX — toggles the slow blue glow that marks when the falling brick can be deleted (◀+▶).
  • Sentence Dots — while a sentence challenge is set, rings each falling brick whose letter is in the sentence with red dots on all four sides. Toggle on/off.
  • Bonus — toggle the backwards-word bonus. See "Backwards-word bonus" above.
  • Lightning — toggle the 3-D lightning, flicker, thunder and camera kick on each solve.
  • Storm Mode — ambient thunderstorm; random strikes roll through between solves.
  • Smoke — toggle the wispy smoke that rises off cleared cells (the orange cooling glow stays either way).
  • Blocks — scatter immovable grey obstacle blocks (a random 5–30% of the board) that bricks collide with and stack on. They flare yellow on arrival and cool to grey. Press again for a fresh random layout.
  • Black Holes — a three-tap cycle. Tap once: dark black-hole blocks appear (up to 20% of the board) and any brick that touches one is devoured — it red-shifts, shrinks, and spirals in. Tap again: their pull widens to 1 cell, so bricks falling nearby gravitate straight into them mid-drop. Tap a third time: the holes vanish and the effect switches off.
  • ▶AI Play — hand your live game to the AI; press Take Over on the badge to take it back. See "Watch the AI play" below.
  • Words — open the dictionary to add, import, or export your own word lists (saved on your device).
  • Sound — toggle audio.

Restricted words (educators)

Type a word into Restricted words to ban it for the game. It will never clear, and any time it appears its bricks glow red — great for steering away from a target spelling.

Sentence challenge

Set a target sentence under the board. Spell each word in the game — in any order — and its chip lights up blue as the board flashes. Spell the whole sentence to win. Any real word clears as normal even when it sits inside a target (FIG inside FIGHT), and the target still counts when you spell it in full. Tip: to complete a long target, finish it in one move — place the last letter that completes the whole word — so a shorter word inside it doesn't clear first. While a sentence is set, any falling brick whose letter appears in the sentence is ringed with small red dots on all four sides so you can spot useful letters at a glance — toggle this with the Sentence Dots button.

Debug Monitor (power users)

Press ` (backtick) to open the Debug / Tutor Monitor — a live diagnostics panel in the top-left. It shows the active difficulty and letter-help, every mode, the falling piece, engine and board stats, and what the tutor is drawing versus what's actually reachable (with a built-in bug self-check). It also keeps a black-box recorder: if an error ever occurs the game auto-pauses and the panel opens so nothing is lost, and you can page back through recent board snapshots or copy the full state. Press ` again to close it. Handy for tuning or reporting anything odd.

Backwards-word bonus ⚡

Spell a word so it reads as a real word only backwards — lay the tiles R‑A‑F and the engine reads FAR — and you earn a bonus. The board pauses, a glowing BONUS banner flashes at the top of the board, and you get to delete any bricks you choose, up to the length of the word you spelled (a 3‑letter word → up to 3 bricks). Tap each brick to vaporize it with the usual tumble‑off animation; press Done (or use them all) to resume. It only fires on a single‑word solve that's valid just backwards — a word that works both ways (TAR/RAT) or any normal forward spelling doesn't count. A clever, hard‑earned way to clear a trouble spot.

Lightning & Storm Mode

Every word you solve cracks a hand‑built 3‑D lightning bolt over the board — it genuinely brightens the scene, flickers the whole UI like a power surge, rolls thunder, and kicks the camera. Toggle the entire spectacle with Lightning. Flip on Storm Mode for an ambient thunderstorm: random strikes roll through on their own between solves.

Cooling embers & smoke

When bricks clear, the cell they left behind flares orange‑hot and cools like real metal — bright amber, then orange, then deep red, fading back into the abyss over a couple of seconds (fast at first, then slow, just like radiating heat). Wispy smoke drifts up off the glow. If a falling tile lands in a still‑glowing cell it simply covers it, like dropping iron onto a cooling forge. The smoke is toggleable with the Smoke button; the ember glow always stays.

Anti-Gravity tuning

In Anti‑Gravity mode a row of extra controls appears so you can tune the physics live:

  • Emitter: Fixed / Random — fire from the edge cell you pick, or randomize the side and cell every brick.
  • Gravity: Off / Low / Med / High — how strongly drifting bricks are pulled.
  • Force: Low / Med / High — how hard bricks shove apart when they collide.
  • Timer: Off / 30s / 15s / 8s — an on‑board countdown that auto‑locks the current brick when it runs out.
  • Float Fade — toggle the slow scatter‑and‑fade animation when words clear.

Watch the AI play — and hand off mid-game

SPELLTRIS can play itself. On the start screen press ▶ Watch Demo and an AI takes over a fresh game — it randomly picks Classic or Anti‑Gravity each run and plays on its own, steering pieces to complete the longest words. It's a real player: it reads the board and drives the very same controls you do, not a special path.

Even better — while you're playing, tap ▶ AI Play in the control bar (it's there in both modes) and the AI takes over your live game, mid‑fall, exactly where you left it: same score, same board, same run. Watch it dig you out, then press Take Over on the on‑screen badge to grab the controls right back and keep playing the same game. Pass it back and forth as many times as you like — change settings mid‑watch and the AI adapts on the fly. Exit returns to the menu. As far as we know, handing one continuous game back and forth between player and AI has never been done in a game before.

Built-In Test (BIT) & the engine logic scan

From the start screen, press BIT to run a one‑click self‑test of the game's own systems — believed to be the first such feature in a commercial game. Borrowed from aviation and industrial hardware, where a machine checks its own instruments at power‑on, BIT runs only on the home screen, animates a pass/fail report, then restores itself to leave zero trace — proven byte‑for‑byte identical before and after. It runs in two layers, 14 checks in total:

  • Recorder self‑test (8 checks) — verifies the black‑box safety net itself: that game events log, board snapshots capture, the rolling buffers cap correctly, a fault is caught and the Debug Monitor auto‑opens, the live panel renders, the incident export is complete, and the frame‑health counters track. In short: is the smoke detector working?
  • Engine logic scan (6 checks) — the headline. BIT spins up the real gameplay engine on a private, in‑memory board and plays controlled micro‑rounds against it using randomized, property‑based (“fuzz”) inputs. Every run is different: a fresh random seed draws random real words (random lengths) from the live dictionary, on randomly‑sized boards, at random positions, sometimes vertical, sometimes reversed. It checks invariants, not fixed answers — “any real word, placed anywhere, must be detected, clear, and let the tiles above settle.” The six cover word validation, line scanning, all‑direction board clearing, gravity, the full clear‑and‑collapse pipeline, and the anti‑gravity placement snap.

Because no two runs test the same case, repeated runs keep widening coverage and raise the odds of flushing out a hidden bug. Failures are reproducible: the seed is shown in the results, and any failure prints the exact word, board, and coordinates it used. The scan was validated over 20,000 randomized runs with zero false failures — and during development it caught a real flaw in a test, exactly the bug‑finding power it's built for. A full run reports ✓ ALL SYSTEMS GO (14/14) in milliseconds, with no servers and no install.

The panels

The left panel tracks the board (fill, danger, letter counts, a live frequency chart) and hosts a tiny YouTube player. The right panel tracks your run — time, words, speed, streaks, the four-direction word compass, and a log of every word you've solved.
